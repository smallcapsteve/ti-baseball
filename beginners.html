Beginners Program · Ages 5–13

Introduction to Baseball.

New to the game? You're in the right place. Our Introduction to Baseball program is built for first-timers — kids who've never picked up a glove, never swung a bat, and don't yet know the difference between a strike and a foul ball. By the end of 8 weeks, they will.

We cap each group at 16 players so every kid gets real reps and individual attention. The energy stays high, and every drill is focused on the fundamentals players will use for the rest of their baseball lives — throwing, catching, hitting off a tee, fielding, baserunning, and the basic rules and flow of the game. Most importantly, we make sure they leave each session smiling and asking when they can come back.

The Curriculum

What Players Learn.

  • Hitting Basics

    Grip, stance, balance, swing path. Off the tee first, then soft toss, then live front toss.

  • Throwing & Catching

    Four-seam grip, throwing mechanics, catching position. Building arm strength safely.

  • Fielding Fundamentals

    Ground balls, fly balls, getting in front of the ball, glove-side and backhand reads.

  • Baserunning

    Running through first, rounding the bases, leading off, tagging up. The "where do I go?" part of the game.

  • Rules & Game Awareness

    Balls, strikes, outs, force-outs, tags, fair vs. foul. The "what just happened?" part of the game.

  • Confidence & Fun

    Every session has games, friendly competition, and high-fives. Kids should leave excited to come back.
